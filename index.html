Types of Mental Disorders: Symptoms and Causes

• Anxiety Disorders

Anxiety disorders cause fear or dread in response to certain objects or situations. Common types include generalized anxiety disorder, panic disorder, and social anxiety disorder.

Symptoms:

  • Tiredness
  • Muscle tension
  • Chest tightness or pain
  • Shortness of breath
  • Rapid heartbeat

Causes:

  • Genetics
  • Stress
  • Environmental factors

• Mood Disorders

Mood disorders involve persistent feelings of sadness or euphoria. Examples include bipolar disorder, depression, and cyclothymic disorder.

Symptoms:

  • Hopelessness
  • Fatigue
  • Rapid mood changes
  • Racing thoughts
  • Risky behaviors

Causes:

  • Genetics
  • Chronic stress
  • Isolation

• Psychotic Disorders

Psychotic disorders distort awareness and thinking. Schizophrenia is a common example.

Symptoms:

  • Hallucinations
  • Delusions
  • Disorganized thoughts

Causes:

  • Stress
  • Drug abuse
  • Major life changes

• Eating Disorders

Eating disorders involve abnormal behaviors around food and weight. Examples include anorexia nervosa and bulimia nervosa.

Symptoms:

  • Dramatic weight changes
  • Excessive exercise
  • Refusal to eat

Causes:

  • Genetic factors
  • Behavioral influences
  • Social pressures

• Personality Disorders

Personality disorders involve unhealthy, inflexible thoughts and behaviors. Examples include borderline and narcissistic personality disorders.

Symptoms:

  • Social anxiety
  • Mood swings
  • Impulsive behaviors

Causes:

  • Genetics
  • Childhood trauma

• Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

PTSD develops after a traumatic experience, such as assault or a natural disaster.

Symptoms:

  • Flashbacks
  • Nightmares
  • Avoidance behaviors

• Impulse Control and Addiction Disorders

These disorders involve irresistible urges to perform harmful behaviors, such as gambling or substance abuse.

Symptoms:

  • Stealing
  • Compulsive lying
  • Ignoring responsibilities

Causes:

  • History of trauma
  • Emotional regulation issues

• Factitious Disorders

Factitious disorder involves falsifying symptoms to appear ill.

Symptoms:

  • Inconsistent medical history
  • Eagerness for medical tests
  • Predicting negative outcomes

Causes:

  • History of child abuse
  • Medical trauma

• Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

OCD involves repetitive thoughts and behaviors that cause significant stress.

Symptoms:

  • Repetitive thoughts
  • Compulsive behaviors
  • Fear of germs

• Sexual and Gender Disorders

These disorders affect sexual desire, performance, and identity.

Symptoms:

  • Conflicts about gender identity
  • Inhibited sexual desire

Causes:

  • Chromosomal abnormalities
  • Hormonal issues
